DELICIOUS PICKLES FROM INDIA

February 19, 2018

Acetic acid (vinegar) is the primary ingredient used in pickle manufacturing. After water, it makes up the bulk of the pickle liquor and contributes significantly to the flavor of the pickle giving it a sour taste.

  • Simply add sugar, a big splash of Tabasco Sauce, dried garlic and red pepper flakes. Let them sit at room temperature for two hours, stirring now and then. During their rest, they will create their own liquid. After the two hours, transfer to jars and let them rest in the refrigerator for three days before eating.

INSTANT CUCUMBER PICKLE

My grandmother is one of those people who get excited about cooking even at her age of seventy-eight. Cooking for her family and loved ones have occupied most of her time, all her life. As a young girl she cooked for her five brothers and two sisters, as a young wife she cooked for her many brothers-in law and sisters-in law apart from her immediate family and she makes amazing delicacies for those dearest to her, her grandchildren.

INGREDIENTS

  • 4 to 5 baby Cucumbers or 2 medium Cucumber
  • 1 tsp salt 2 tsp red chilli powder
  • ¼ tsp fenugreek seed (methi) powder
  • ¼ tsp asafetida
  • ¼ tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • juice of 1 small lime

INSTRUCTIONS:

  1. Slice the cucumber and mix with all the ingredients. Can be kept refrigerated for a week.

MANGO JAM

Mango Jam can be made with any variety of mangoes. Semi-ripe mangoes work best, but either raw or ripe mangoes may be used.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 pounds ripe mangoes
  • 1 ½ cups white sugar
  • ¾ cup water
  • 3 saffron threads (Optional)

INSTRUCTIONS:

  1. Boil, steam, or microwave the whole mangoes until soft. Cool, then remove the peel and inner seed; place the mango pulp in a large bowl. Use a fork or potato masher to mash the pulp well.
  2. Place the sugar and water in a large saucepan over low heat, stir mixture, and bring to a boil. When mixture begins boiling, increase heat to medium-high. Continue boiling until fine, soft threads form, 270 degrees F (135 degrees C). Stir in the mango pulp, add the saffron threads, if desired, and boil until the mixture thickens, about 5 minutes.
  3. Pour cooked jam into sterilized jars and seal according to canning directions.
